Undergraduate Thesis

A Novel Approach for Developing Efficient and Convenient Short Assessments to Approximate a Long Assessment

Supervisor: Kang Lee, Department of Applied Psychology and Human Development, University of Toronto


The goal of this thesis is to develop a self-assessment tool to efficiently predict the likelihood of anxiety, using a machine learning approach. The proliferation of anxiety among young adults today is a serious mental health concern, and existing anxiety assessment tools are either too long or not very accessible. Research work first need be done by applying various machine learning techniques on datasets of existing anxiety and mental health questionnaires, before selecting the best approach. Then, a best set of survey questions and machine learning models would be selected, which would accurately predict the likelihood of anxiety, based on how the questions were answered. Finally, a prototype of the thesis product would be implemented as a web application, with a web user interface that mimic the functionality of the anxiety self-assessment tool.

This paper has been published in the Behavior Research Methods journal.



Masters Thesis

Rapid Assessment of Psychiatric Disorder Risks Using Machine Learning

Supervisor: Kang Lee, Department of Applied Psychology and Human Development, University of Toronto


This study applies a set of machine learning methods to develop a shortened MMPI-2 scale, which consists of 567 items, and evaluate its validity. We first selected the most important items using feature importance measures such as MRMR and SHAP, and then trained machine learning models on the selected items. We employed a novel machine learning method called the stacked generalization ensemble, of several machine learning techniques to achieve the above accuracy. We were able to reduce the length of the MMPI-2 by over 82%. The shortened MMPI scales can measure the 10 target conditions/subscales with at least 85% Area Under Curve of the Receiver Operating Characteristic (AUC ROC). Finally, we implemented this shortened MMPI-2 scale into a mobile-friendly web application, to demonstrate its effectiveness when using it for regular mental health monitoring or screening. This shortened scale can help alleviate pressure on healthcare systems for dealing with mental health concerns, as well as demonstrating a novel approach for developing cognitive tests.
