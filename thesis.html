Undergraduate Thesis

A Novel Approach for Developing Efficient and Convenient Short Assessments to Approximate a Long Assessment

Supervisor: Kang Lee, Ontario Institute for Studies in Education, University of Toronto

September 2020 — April 2021


The goal of this thesis is to develop a self-assessment tool to efficiently predict the likelihood of anxiety, using a machine learning approach. The proliferation of anxiety among young adults today is a serious mental health concern, and existing anxiety assessment tools are either too long or not very accessible. Research work first need be done by applying various machine learning techniques on datasets of existing anxiety and mental health questionnaires, before selecting the best approach. Then, a best set of survey questions and machine learning models would be selected, which would accurately predict the likelihood of anxiety, based on how the questions were answered. Finally, a prototype of the thesis product would be implemented as a web application, with a web user interface that mimic the functionality of the anxiety self-assessment tool.

This paper has been published in the Behavior Research Methods journal.

Masters Thesis

Rapid Assessment of Psychiatric Disorder Risks Using Machine Learning

Supervisor: Kang Lee, Ontario Institute for Studies in Education, University of Toronto

September 2022 — April 2023


The Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory (MMPI) is a test of personality and psychopathology with many applications (Camara et al., 2000) such as the diagnostic and treatment of mental disorders, forensic psychology, and screening in personnel selection (Butcher and Williams, 2009). It is the most widely used psychometric test in the world, and its most commonly administered version, the MMPI-2, contains 567 true/false questions (Drayton, 2009). However, completing the test is exhausting and time consuming due to the large number of questions. This makes the test difficult and inconvenient to be taken on a regular basis or to be administered on a large scale.
